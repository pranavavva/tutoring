Course Structure

This section only applies to the Full Course (12-week program). If you choose only specifc Test Sections, please refer to those sections here.

After the free 30-min consultation, you will take a full practice test under test-day conditions (observing all time constraints and breaks). This will serve as a diagnotic test. After you take this initial test, you will send me your results; I will then analyze them, determine exactly where your strengths and weaknesses are, and develop a lesson plan just for you. We will break down all the subject material on the SAT into 3 groups: Reading, Writing, and Math. We will cover each Test over four weeks, spending one week on each major subcategory within that Test. After every four weeks, you will take a timed test to assess your improvement.

Weeks 1-4 Reading Test
  • Command of Evidence (Week 1)
    • How does the author support their arguments?
    • How can the passage be used to support arguments?
    • How do graphs and charts relate to the passage?
  • Words in Context (Week 2)
    • What is the meaning of words and phrases used in the context of the passage?
    • How does word choice affect the tone, mood, and style of the passage?
  • Analysis in History/Social Studies and Science (Week 3)
    • How does the passage relate to topics in History/Social Studies and Science?
    • How can you apply scientific principles to charts and graphs presented with the article?

After Week 3, you will take a timed Reading Test section. We will review your improvements and cover final questions and test-taking strategies in Week 4.

Weeks 5-8 Writing Test
  • Command of Evidence (Week 5)
    • Improve how the passage develops ideas
  • Words in Context (Week 5)
    • Improve word choice
  • Analysis in History/Social Studies and Science (Week 6)
    • Improve the passage as it relates to History and Science subjects
  • Expression of Ideas (Week 6)
    • Improve the organization of the passage
  • Standard English Convention (Week 7)
    • Improve passage based on “basic” grammar rules

After Week 8, you will take timed Reading and Writing Test sections. We will review your improvements and cover final questions and test-taking strategies in Week 8.

Weeks 9-12 Math Test
  • Heart of Algebra (Week 9)
    • Covers most of Algebra I
    • Linear equations and linear systems
  • Problem Solving and Data Analysis (Week 9)
    • Covers most of Statistics, as well as some Algebra I and II
    • Statistical analysis
  • Passport to Advanced Math (Week 10)
    • Covers most of Algebra II
    • Manipulations of complex and non-linear equations and systems
  • Additional Topics in Math (Week 11)
    • Covers most of Geometry and Trigonometry

After Week 11, you will take a full SAT practice test, similar to the diagnostic test. This acts as a final review of everything you've learned over the past 12 weeks. We will review your improvements and cover final questions and test-taking tips in Week 12.

Test-taking strategies, as well as how to tie them to subject-matter knowledge, will be taught throughout the course. Homework will mainly consist of questions taught during the previous week, along with material taught in previous weeks. This is to continually reinforce newly-taught material. Homework questions will be reviewed at the beginning of each class. As noted above, homework for Weeks 3, 7, and 11 will be timed practice sections.
